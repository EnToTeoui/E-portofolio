Week 1-3 (Discussion):
In week 1-3 we had a collaborative discussion regarding the impact of industry 4.0 and industry 5.0 on the sector we are involved or interested in. This discussion allowed me to understand better the Industry 4.0 and 5.0 as well as the benefits and drawbacks for each one of them. This can be a big change in the Data Science field regarding the privacy and ethical concerns this field faces. This is my summary post regarding the topic we had and my thoughts on it.
"Metacalf (2024) classifies Industry 5.0 as the evolution of Industry 4.0 whereas in Industry 4.0 the main focus was the efficiency of the automated systems but for Industry 5.0 a more human-centric approach between people and intelligent systems will be held. Regarding the AI and deep learning sector, this highlights the concerns of many people for ethical governance, privacy issues and transparency of the industries designing and deploying these AI technologies in public. Whilst Industry 4.0 brought major advances through automation, big data, and deep learning, it showcased risks associated with privacy breaches and system fragility (Bai et al., 2023). These concerns became more alarming after the OpenAI’s ChatGPT incident in 2023 where it suffered a data leak caused by a Redis caching bug exposing payment details and users’ chat histories (OpenAI, 2023). Industry 5.0 uphold embedding human oversight and ethical accountability into AI system design (Metacalf, 2024). This means that for deep learning applications the main priority would be the safety and resilience of the systems over performance."

Week 8-10 (Discussion):
In week 8-10 we had a collaborative discussion regarding our thoughts on the risks and benefits of the use of AI writers at different levels, from administrative work to creative writing. This discussion enabled us to discuss and view different perspectives regarding the issues and improve our communication skills by interacting with our peers. In addition, it showcased us that over committing to AI usage can harm one of humans great abilities, creativity. This is my summary post regarding the topic we had and my thoughts on it.
"Hutson (2021) talks about the rise of AI “robo-writers”, highlighting their advancement in producing fluent human-like text. AI writing tools have proved to be really efficient but also raise ethical concerns across administrative and creative domains. In administrative work, AI can automate reports, summarize documents and manage correspondence, allowing human workers to focus on strategic and interpersonal tasks (Davenport & Bean, 2022). These applications help with productivity and consistency whilst reducing operational costs. On the other hand, fully relying on AI generated content risks deskilling administrative roles and embedding biases from training data into official communication (Bender et al., 2021). In creative writing, AI can be helpful with boosting creativity by offering new ideas and enhancing proposals. However, this raises concerns regarding originality and questions regarding authenticity (Hutson, 2021). In addition, if creativity becomes more data-driven humans could start seeing a decrease in their creativity which would raise real concerns about the topic. In the end, the challenge is not if AI should be used in such cases but how can we implement it with minimized consequences. This could be achieved with human oversight and transparent disclosure of such AI implementations to make sure it is enhancing human creativity and does not mask human creativity."
