Summary (Deciphering Big Data)

General view: In this module I managed to grow as a person and gain a deeper understanding of the Data Science field. It helped me work on my critical thinking and analysis as well as my problem-solving skills. In addition, due to the group project we have had, I was able to work on my communication skills, as well as my teamwork and leadership. In this module I learned to identify and manage challenges, security issues and risks, limitations and opportunities for data wrangling. It helped me critically analyse data wrangling problems and determine appropriate methodologies tools and techniques to solve them. It helped me design, develop and evaluate solutions for processing datasets and solving complex problems in various environments. Lastly, I was able to develop and implement the skills required to be an effective member of a development team in virtual professional environment.

Week (1-3): Data wrangling is essential when working with raw datasets, for the user to be able and work with it, however it can be challenging to prepare facing some challenges, security risks and limitations during the process. Some challenges you can face throughout the process is raw data usually is filled with errors, inconsistent datatypes and missing values. Another challenge is working with big datasets can be time consuming and needs big storage space to be saved at. There are also some security risks whilst data wrangling, as working with large unfiltered datasets can lead to data privacy breaches or sensitive information may get stored in unsecure files and be vulnerable to malicious activities. Furthermore, there are also some limitations to data wrangling as some big data sets require a certain amount of time to be dealt with and can be time consuming. An additional limitation to large datasets is that some more user friendly software for data wrangling are not compatible with handling large datasets. Despite all these setbacks that can be faced whilst data wrangling, it provides datasets that can be much easier analysed and preserved for use. They can also be stored and used more than once which can save valuable time in long term.

Week (4-5): Data Management Pipeline is essentialyly the step-by-step process of converting raw data to usable data, meaning that the data can be analysed and reported upon. Data Management Pipeline usually consists of 6 steps; Data Collection, Data Cleaning, Data Transformation, Data storage, Data Analysis and Reporting the data which is the final step. Initially you collect the data from a source, then you clean and transform the data by removing any duplicates, sorting out missing values, data types and transforming it into an easy to analysis dataset. Then you have to make sure that the dataset is stored somewhere safe and it can retain its integrity. And finally, you analyse the dataset with software producing a report that can help the people involved make more effective and efficient decisions regarding the dataset.

Week (7): Normalisation is used to prevent any unwanted errors or anomalies in the data. It usually has 3 normal forms and is supposed to follow certain rules to achieve those normal forms. for the first normal form you need atomic values and each row to be unique. For the second normal form, the data needs to be in first normal form and also every column that is not a part of a primary key to be depended on the primary key. And for the third normal form the data must be in the second normal form and every column that is not part of the primary key to be dependent only on the primary key itself.

Week (8-10): General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a European Union regulation that protects the personal data of EU citizens and provides individuals with control and security regarding their sensitive information. In Cyprus as a member of the European Union the GDPR regulations are fully implemented and in addition there are even some additional regulations set by the country Data Protection Law. Individuals in Cyprus are permitted to know what data organizations are storing regarding them, they are eligible to request a deletion of their data and limit how their data is being used. In addition they are permitted to refuse for their data to be processed for certain cases. Cyprus has a department regarding GDPR regulations and the national law for data protection and violating these laws can cost up to 10 million or 20 million euros in fines.
